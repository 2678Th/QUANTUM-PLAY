A browser-based visual simulation of one of quantum physics’ strangest ideas: particles can form wave-like interference patterns, until measurement changes what we see.

What this shows

When the observer is off, the simulation places detections according to a wave-like interference pattern. When the observer is on, detections cluster behind the slits, representing particle-like behaviour after measurement.

This is an educational visual model, not a laboratory-accurate quantum physics engine. Its purpose is to make the core idea understandable and interactive.
